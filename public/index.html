Exploring Natural Gas for Rick Road

Over the past several months, a group of Rick Road residents has been working with PECO to explore the possibility of bringing natural gas service to our neighborhood.

PECO is currently evaluating whether natural gas service can be extended to Rick Road.

What Is Happening Right Now?

As the next step in the evaluation process, PECO is gathering non-binding applications from homeowners within the proposed project area.

According to PECO, every completed non-binding application helps reduce the project’s cost and helps PECO evaluate the opportunity for Rick Road.

How Does My Application Help?

According to PECO, each completed non-binding application helps reduce the project’s cost by allowing PECO to account for the long-term value of providing natural gas service to that property.

In simple terms, the more neighbors who submit an application, the lower the project cost becomes.

Potential Example

With minimal participation, the estimated cost to bring natural gas to an individual property could exceed:

$13,600+per home

If all households within the proposed project area submit a non-binding application, the estimated project cost could potentially be reduced by approximately:

50%potential cost reduction

The Bottom Line

The more neighbors who submit an application, the lower the project cost becomes.

A few minutes today can benefit the entire neighborhood.

Potential Rebates & Savings

If the project ultimately moves forward, PECO currently offers rebates and energy-efficiency programs for qualifying natural gas equipment and upgrades.

Examples may include:

  • Up to $1,000 for qualifying high-efficiency combo boilers
  • Up to $600 for qualifying high-efficiency furnaces
  • Up to $400 for qualifying tankless water heaters
  • $200 heating conversion rebate
  • Discounted smart thermostats
  • Low-cost home energy assessments with free energy-saving products installed
